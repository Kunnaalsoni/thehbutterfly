Is 2020 the worst year yet?

2020 is certainly a year that no human on this earth will ever forget. However, labelling it as the worst year yet would not be justifiable because there have been a few years even worst than 2020. For instance, in 1349, Black Death wiped off almost half of Europe’s population. How can we forget Spanish Flu that wreaked havoc in the year 1918 by claiming lives of more than 50 million people? By regressing, we would find out that in the year 1520, smallpox claimed lives of more than 60% people of the original dwellers of the continent of America. If you are still not convinced then probably, a few chapters from the history needs to be turned to learn about an unimaginable phase. This period is still believed to be the worst phase in history so far in the context of the human struggles that seemed endless. The phase is none other than the year 536 AD.

It embarked with a dense fog that earlier seemed like any other normal fog like Covid-19 seemed like just any other normal virus. Nevertheless, in no time, it began to engulf countries and continents one after another, triggering a nightmare for the dwellers from Europe, several parts of Asia and the Middle East. It is difficult to imagine that the fog plunged a few continents into darkness for almost 2 years while affecting the countries all across the globe in some way or the other. It resulted in the coldest decade, famine, failed crops, starvation, economic disaster and whatnot.

To conclude, 2020 cannot be labelled as the worst year yet, owing to the myriad reasons. In the wake of technological advancement, many jobs thrive on the internet and hence have been fortunately unaffected by the negative repercussions of the pandemic. Indeed, we are fortunate that despite the minimal physical movement, with the help of myriad dynamic communication mediums, we have not completely lost touch with our near and dear ones. There have been a few businesses that have cemented their positioning in the market during this time. Undeniably, it has been unexpectedly quite a rough phase in terms of the global recession, plummeting stock market but at the same time, the fact cannot be denied that every crisis has a silver lining.

The power we discover inside ourselves as we survive a life-threatening experience can be utilized equally well outside of crisis, too. I am, in every moment, capable of mustering the strength to survive again—or of tapping that strength in other good, productive, healthy ways.
